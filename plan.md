# Plan of action

1. Took a video
2. Took the most interesting frames of the video (where the model would see a new section of the house/yard)
  a. this was around every 2-3s
3. Used those frames in the LTX Video 0.9.8 13B model on Wan2GP
4. Used an AI generated prompt to describe the scene, kept it general by describing the sky, house, ground, etc and not focussing on the details (e.g., flowers)

Prompt

```markdown
A quiet suburban residential setting on a clear sunny day with a bright blue sky. The environment consists of detached houses with brick and light-colored facades, tiled roofs, and rectangular windows with white frames and curtains. In the foreground is a wide gravel driveway or open parking area with uneven texture, small stones, and subtle tire marks. On the left side, there are neatly trimmed hedges casting soft shadows, and a sidewalk with modern street lamps extending into the distance along a quiet road. The scene is completely static with no moving people, vehicles, or objects. Lighting remains consistent, with strong sunlight creating defined shadows on the ground and building walls. The camera moves smoothly and naturally through the space at a steady pace, translating laterally and slightly forward, as if handheld or on a slow dolly, maintaining a consistent height and perspective. The motion reveals more of the driveway, the side of the house, and adjacent gardens while preserving spatial continuity. No abrupt movements, distortions, or changes in the environment—only continuous, realistic camera motion through a still scene.
```

5. 5 videos were generated by the model. The end of each video was the end-frame chosen. The start frame of the 2 until last video was the last frame of the previous video. This took 29m 15s.
6. Combined all these videos into one using ffmpeg
7. Used this final video in the LichtFeld-Studio pipeline
